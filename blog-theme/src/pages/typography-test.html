Why Typography Matters

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. The arrangement of type involves selecting typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing.

Typography is two-dimensional architecture, based on experience and imagination, and guided by rules and readability.

Key Principles

  • Use a clear hierarchy
  • Maintain consistent spacing
  • Choose appropriate font pairings
  • Ensure sufficient contrast

Responsive Typography

Resize your browser window to see how typography adapts across different screen sizes. The design maintains readability on mobile (min 16px body), tablet, and desktop viewports.

Mobile First Approach

All font sizes are optimized for mobile devices first, ensuring excellent readability on smaller screens. As the viewport increases, typography scales appropriately to take advantage of larger displays.
